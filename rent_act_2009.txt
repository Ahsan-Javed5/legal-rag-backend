Section 3: Application. This Act applies to all rented premises in Punjab except cantonment areas and premises let at monthly rent exceeding PKR 100,000.

Section 4: Rent Controller. The government shall appoint Rent Controllers for each city/town. All applications under this Act shall be filed before Rent Controller.

Section 5: Written agreement mandatory. Every tenancy shall be reduced to writing. The agreement shall contain names of parties, description of premises, monthly rent, security deposit, and duration.

Section 6: Rent limit at commencement. No landlord shall demand rent exceeding agreed amount. Rent for first 3 years cannot be increased unless agreed in writing.

Section 7: Annual rent increase. After first 3 years, landlord may increase rent once per year by giving 60 days written notice. Maximum increase per year is 10% of existing rent.

Section 8: Security deposit. Landlord may take security deposit not exceeding 2 months rent. Deposit must be returned within 30 days of tenant vacating premises. Deduction allowed only for damage beyond normal wear and tear.

Section 9: Receipt mandatory. Landlord shall issue written receipt for every rent payment. Tenant can demand receipt within 7 days.

Section 10: Landlord obligations. Landlord shall keep premises fit for habitation. Repair roof, water supply, electricity wiring, and sanitation facilities. If landlord fails, tenant may repair and deduct from rent.

Section 11: Tenant obligations. Tenant shall pay rent on time, use premises for agreed purpose, not cause nuisance, and inform landlord of required repairs.

Section 12: Entry by landlord. Landlord cannot enter premises without 24 hours written notice except for emergency (fire, gas leak, flood). Entry shall be between 9 AM to 6 PM only.

Section 13: Subletting prohibited. Tenant cannot sublet premises without landlord's written consent. Unauthorized subletting is ground for eviction.

Section 14: Eviction grounds. Rent Controller may order eviction if tenant: (a) fails to pay rent for 3 consecutive months, (b) uses premises for illegal purpose, (c) causes nuisance, (d) sublets without consent, (e) damages property.

Section 15: Notice for eviction. Landlord shall give 30 days written notice before filing eviction application. Notice shall state grounds clearly.

Section 16: Tenant protection. No landlord shall cut off electricity, water, or gas supply to force eviction. Such act is punishable with fine up to PKR 50,000.

Section 17: Rent increase dispute. If tenant disagrees with rent increase, tenant may apply to Rent Controller within 30 days. Rent Controller decides fair rent after hearing both parties.

Section 18: Vacating premises. Tenant shall vacate within 15 days of eviction order. If not vacated, landlord may approach Rent Controller for possession through bailiff.

Section 19: Appeal. Any party aggrieved by Rent Controller's order may appeal to District Judge within 30 days.

Section 20: Overriding effect. This Act overrides any inconsistent agreement between landlord and tenant. Any clause in agreement contrary to this Act is void.
